- [“You Had One Job”: Why Twenty Years of DevOps Has Failed to Do it | Charity Majors on Honeycomb](https://www.honeycomb.io/blog/you-had-one-job-why-twenty-years-of-devops-has-failed-to-do-it) - The DevOps movement has not yet bridged the gap between developers and operations as the tooling requires expertise that most developers lack. The author posits that AI may help bridge this gap as LLMs have been trained on standard instrumentation practices
- [Ghostty AI Usage Policy | ghostty-org/ghostty on Github](https://github.com/ghostty-org/ghostty/blob/main/AI_POLICY.md) - ["If you're using low-effort AI to create low-effort content, I have no human obligation to help you."](https://github.com/ghostty-org/ghostty/commit/f00de7ee4b9bbc5a0fb5f4c06755780c773a0f5e). Following a surge in low-quality contributions Ghostty will now only accept AI-generated contributions for accepted issues and maintainers. Any users pushing low-quality AI submissions will also be banned from contributing further

But not all opportunities are equal. When assessing opportunities prioritise those that are visible and those that have a broader impact
- [How to Provide Great Feedback When You’re Not In Charge | fs.blog](https://fs.blog/provide-feedback/) - There's three kinds of feedback: appreciation, advice and evaluation. It's just as important to offer appreciation as it is to provide advice, and you should endeavour to be specific when providing either so that the recipient can better understand what they should change
